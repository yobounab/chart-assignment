The N.Y.P.D. Says New York City is Safer, But What Does the Data Actually Say?

By Youcef O. Bounab

The number of shooting victims in New York City appears to be decreasing, compared to data from last year, according to the N.Y.P.D., whose officers ramp up citywide gun arrests.

“The department is seeing positive progress in our decrease in shootings across the city,” N.Y.P.D. Chief of Department Rodney Harrison said on Wednesday, Nov. 3, in a virtual press conference.

But while Harrison congratulated officers and commissioners, data show that the decrease in the number of shooting victims is actually 1% only.

While shootings in Brooklyn, Queens and Staten Island certainly saw a drop, the borough of the Bronx has had an alarming increase of 22% as of last month, compared to the previous year during the same period.

“The Bronx is the only borough in the city that has over 1,000 gun arrests year-to-date,” Chief Harrison explained.

All citywide increases, however, took place during the first half of the year —a pattern that only came about because of the Covid-19 pandemic and quarantine. For instance, the month of April saw a rise of 153% of shooting victims in 2021, and March 85.5%.

While Chief Harrison credited this “positive progress” to the N.Y.P.D., he also called on community members to be more involved in neighborhood policing.

“We need to hear your complaints so we can continue to make this a safe city,” he said.
